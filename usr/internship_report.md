## 1. INTRODUCTION
This report documents the experience, tasks, and learnings acquired during the internship at SIGNATURE as a Designing & Marketing Intern. The internship provided hands-on experience in custom denim design, digital marketing, and the end-to-end process of translating customer requirements into personalized fashion products.

## 2. COMPANY PROFILE
SIGNATURE is a brand specializing in customized denim designs. The business model revolves around capturing customer requirements and translating their personal vision into unique, tailored denim pieces through various techniques such as embroidery, distressing, patch-working, laser engraving, and screen printing.

## 3. OBJECTIVES OF THE INTERNSHIP
- To understand the business model of a custom fashion brand.
- To learn the technical processes of denim customization (sketching, digitizing, distressing, etc.).
- To develop digital marketing skills, specifically product photography, social media management, and digital design.
- To bridge the gap between design aesthetics and marketing communication.

## 4. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES
As a Designing & Marketing Intern, the primary responsibilities included:
- Collecting and interpreting customer design briefs.
- Creating flat technical sketches and mood boards.
- Designing embroidery layouts, patches, and graphic prints.
- Editing product photos and designing marketing creatives.
- Analyzing social media engagement and developing promotional materials.

## 5. DAILY LOG

**Day 1**
- **Observation:** Got introduced to Signature's business model and how customer requirements are translated into customised denim designs.
- **Learning:** Understood how design plays the central role in a customisation brand where every product is built around a customer's personal vision.

**Day 2**
- **Observation:** Studied Signature's existing design portfolio — past customised denim pieces, patterns, and styles delivered to customers.
- **Learning:** Learned how reviewing past work helps a designer understand the brand's aesthetic standards and range of customisation capabilities.

**Day 3**
- **Observation:** Observed how customer design briefs are collected — including style preferences, colour choices, and embellishment requests.
- **Learning:** Understood that accurately interpreting a customer brief is the most critical first step in the design process for a customisation brand.

**Day 4**
- **Observation:** Studied the basic elements of denim design — cuts, silhouettes, rises, and leg openings — and how they affect the overall look.
- **Learning:** Learned how each design element contributes to the fit and style of the final product and must align with the customer's body type and preference.

**Day 5**
- **Observation:** Practiced sketching basic denim silhouettes by hand to visualise customisation ideas before moving to digital tools.
- **Learning:** Understood that hand sketching is a fast and effective way to explore design concepts and communicate ideas clearly before execution.

**Day 6**
- **Observation:** Got introduced to Adobe Illustrator and practiced using it to create flat technical sketches of denim designs.
- **Learning:** Learned how flat sketches in Illustrator serve as precise technical references for the production team during the customisation process.

**Day 7**
- **Observation:** Created a flat sketch of a customer-requested denim jacket design with specific pocket placement and stitch detailing.
- **Learning:** Understood how detailed technical sketches reduce miscommunication between the design and production teams.

**Day 8**
- **Observation:** Studied how colour palettes are selected for denim customisation based on customer preferences and current fashion trends.
- **Learning:** Learned how colour choice impacts the overall mood of a denim piece and must complement the customer's requested style and embellishments.

**Day 9**
- **Observation:** Practiced creating mood boards for customisation concepts using fabric swatches, colour references, and design inspirations.
- **Learning:** Understood that mood boards help align the designer's vision with the customer's expectations before production begins.

**Day 10**
- **Observation:** Studied different embroidery design styles — floral, geometric, typographic — and how they are applied on denim.
- **Learning:** Learned how embroidery design style must match the overall aesthetic of the denim piece and suit the placement area on the garment.

**Day 11**
- **Observation:** Created an embroidery design layout for a customer order specifying pattern, thread colours, and placement on the denim jacket.
- **Learning:** Understood that a clear embroidery layout prevents errors during execution and ensures the final output matches the customer's vision.

**Day 12**
- **Observation:** Observed how patch designs are conceptualised, drawn, and planned for placement on denim jeans and jackets.
- **Learning:** Learned how patch placement must consider the garment's proportions and the customer's aesthetic preference for a balanced design outcome.

**Day 13**
- **Observation:** Practiced designing custom graphic prints intended for screen printing on denim using Adobe Illustrator.
- **Learning:** Understood how print designs must be created in vector format with limited colours to ensure clean and cost-effective screen printing output.

**Day 14**
- **Observation:** Studied how distressing patterns are planned and marked on denim before manual execution by the production team.
- **Learning:** Learned that distressing must be intentionally designed — not random — to achieve a visually balanced and aesthetically pleasing worn effect.

**Day 15**
- **Observation:** Assisted in designing a full custom denim look for a customer combining wash, distressing, embroidery, and patch elements.
- **Learning:** Understood how combining multiple customisation techniques requires careful design planning to ensure all elements work harmoniously together.

**Day 16**
- **Observation:** Studied how typography is used in denim design for text-based embroidery, prints, and laser engravings.
- **Learning:** Learned that font selection in fashion design must balance readability, style, and compatibility with the chosen application technique.

**Day 17**
- **Observation:** Practiced digitising an embroidery design using embroidery software to prepare it for machine execution.
- **Learning:** Understood how digitisation converts design artwork into stitch paths and determines the final quality of the embroidered output.

**Day 18**
- **Observation:** Observed how design revisions are handled when a customer requests changes to an approved design before production begins.
- **Learning:** Learned that maintaining a flexible and organised design revision process is key to ensuring customer satisfaction in a customisation business.

**Day 19**
- **Observation:** Studied how laser engraving designs are prepared — adjusting contrast, line weight, and file format for machine compatibility.
- **Learning:** Understood that laser-ready design files require specific technical preparation to achieve precise and clean engraving results on denim.

**Day 20**
- **Observation:** Designed a custom label and branding tag layout for finished denim products as part of Signature's product identity.
- **Learning:** Learned how branded labels and tags reinforce the premium and personalised nature of Signature's products at the point of delivery.

**Day 21**
- **Observation:** Studied how seasonal design themes are developed to inspire new customisation offerings for upcoming collections.
- **Learning:** Understood that proactive seasonal design planning keeps the brand's catalogue fresh and gives customers new concepts to explore.

**Day 22**
- **Observation:** Created a design reference sheet compiling colour combinations, stitch styles, and embellishment options for customer consultations.
- **Learning:** Learned that a well-organised design reference guide speeds up the customer brief process and helps customers make more informed design decisions.

**Day 23**
- **Observation:** Practiced creating repeat surface patterns in Illustrator that could be used for custom lining or pocket design in denim products.
- **Learning:** Understood how repeat pattern design adds a unique personalised detail to denim interiors, elevating the overall product experience.

**Day 24**
- **Observation:** Studied how hand-painted design concepts are planned — choosing paint types, brush techniques, and design placement on denim.
- **Learning:** Learned that hand-painting requires both a detailed design plan and knowledge of fabric-safe materials to achieve a lasting and vibrant result.

**Day 25**
- **Observation:** Assisted in preparing a complete design package for a customer order including sketch, colour spec, embroidery layout, and print file.
- **Learning:** Understood that a complete and organised design package ensures every department has clear instructions to execute the customisation accurately.

**Day 26**

- **Learning:** Learned how thoughtful product styling highlights the design details of customised denim and makes the photography more impactful for marketing use.

**Day 27**

**Soft Skills & Marketing:**
- Client brief interpretation and design communication.
